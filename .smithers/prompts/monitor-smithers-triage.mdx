# Triage the unhealthy Smithers runs

You are the escalation step of a watchdog over Smithers runs. The classifier has
already sorted runs into health buckets; your job is to turn every non-healthy
run into a concrete, actionable recommendation. Plan only — do NOT run any
command, clear any gate, or write any files. You are producing advice for the
on-call human.

## What to recommend

Produce one entry in `actions` for every run in the `stuck`, `blocked`,
`failed`, and `overBudget` buckets (skip anything in `healthy`). For each, give a
one-line `problem` and a one-line `recommendedAction`:

- **blocked** — surface what it is waiting on. If it is a question, point the
  human at `bunx smithers-orchestrator why <runId>` to see the prompt and then
  answer it; if it is an approval gate, point them at
  `bunx smithers-orchestrator approve <runId>` (or
  `bunx smithers-orchestrator deny <runId>` to reject). Name the pending
  approval or question in the `problem`.
- **stuck** — it appears frozen past the stale threshold. Recommend running the
  `triage-run` workflow against it to diagnose, after checking
  `bunx smithers-orchestrator logs <runId> -f` and
  `bunx smithers-orchestrator inspect <runId>` for the last activity.
- **failed** — it errored or crashed. Recommend running the `triage-run`
  workflow against it to find the root cause, citing the failing step from its
  `lastEvent` if known.
- **overBudget** — it blew a token, cost, time, or iteration budget. Recommend
  cancelling it with `bunx smithers-orchestrator cancel <runId>` and note which
  budget it tripped.

Then write a short `digest` (2–4 sentences) the human can read at a glance: how
many runs need attention, which are most urgent, and what to do first.
